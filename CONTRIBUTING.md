# Contributing to our Companion Repository

Thank you for helping to grow our **Royal Society paper's companion repository**! We welcome any pull requests.

## What papers and resources to add

- Newly published papers (conferences, journals, workshops), preprints, theses, or patents on conformal prediction for large language models.
- Any codebases, posters, presentation slides, videos, project websites, reviews, or supplementary materials associated with these papers.
- Please do not add any standalone resources (codebases, posters, videos etc.)—there must be a paper/preprint/thesis/patent associated.

## How to add a paper and resources

1. [Fork](https://docs.github.com/en/pull-requests/collaborating-with-pull-requests/working-with-forks/fork-a-repo) this repository.
2. Add your entry to the appropriate category in the `README.md`, keeping the existing format. Make sure to check it's not a duplicate!
   
   ```markdown
   | MM/YYYY | Paper title | [[Paper](https://link)] [[Code](https://link)] |
   ```
   Please do not create any new categories. If you don't believe the work fits in any category, [open an issue](https://docs.github.com/en/issues/tracking-your-work-with-issues/using-issues/creating-an-issue)! You may add as many associated resources as you want to the paper entry. They should align with the following convention (exceptions are allowed! Give your reasoning in the PR): `[Paper] [Preprint] [Workshop] [Thesis] [Patent] [Poster] [Slides] [Video] [Website] [Reviews] [Code]`


3. Keep entries in chronological order by publishing date (not submittance or acceptance date!) of the paper/preprint/thesis/patent, whichever is most recent. For example, if adding both an earlier workshop paper and a journal article, the published date should be that of the journal article.
4. [Open a pull request](https://docs.github.com/en/pull-requests/collaborating-with-pull-requests/proposing-changes-to-your-work-with-pull-requests/creating-a-pull-request-from-a-fork) with a short description of the work you added and why it belongs in the companion repository.

## Quality guidelines for paper resources 

- The papers you add should align with our review scope from our Royal Society paper. This means that works excluded from our review, such as non-conformal prediction papers, encoder-only language models such as BERT, or non-English works, **should not** be added to the repository.
- We **prefer** canonical sources (arXiv, journal website or conference proceedings, GitHub repositories, OpenReview, Google Patents) over mirrors.
- Paywalled-only content is allowed, but open-access alternates provided where possible are **highly encouraged**!
- One pull request per paper entry added where possible—small PRs get **merged faster**!

## Code of conduct

We welcome anyone to make an addition to our companion repository. By contributing, you agree to:

* **Be respectful:** Treat others with kindness and respect in discussions.
* **Keep it constructive:** Focus on improving the papers and resources list and helping others.
* **Avoid harassment:** Trolling, personal attacks, and derogatory language will not be tolerated.

The authors reserve the right to remove any content that violate these guidelines.
